# Prereg: Earnings-reaction drift, PEAD price-proxy (H2)

**Date locked:** 2026-07-12 (before any H2 run)
**Family:** H2 (docs/HYPOTHESES.md)
**Status:** LOCKED

## Mechanism

After an earnings surprise, prices underreact: limited-attention investors and
anchored analysts incorporate the news over days-to-weeks, not minutes — the
best-documented anomaly in the literature (PEAD, five decades of evidence, shrinking but
alive). We proxy the surprise from price/volume alone: a volume-confirmed first-reaction
session in the top decile of trailing reactions marks a genuine positive surprise, and we
are paid by the slow money still catching up after day 1. We enter strictly *after* the
report — the counterparty is the underreacting holder who sells too early into the drift,
never the pre-announcement gambler.

## Universe & data

- Roster: the finalized 250-name study roster (`StudyStore.load_all()`); SPY required as
  the master session axis. Earnings dates from `cache/catalysts/earnings.json`
  (past dates only — future/estimated dates never usable). The runner prints the event
  funnel (total reports → dropped pre-decile → dropped insufficient-comparison → top/bottom
  counts) as the coverage report HYPOTHESES.md asks for; it is diagnostic, never judged.
- OOS wall: **2024-01-01, immutable** (decisions.md re-ratification, newest entry).
  Reaction events and deciles are built over the full roster history (causally); only
  events with signal_date ≥ 2024-01-01 are traded/judged. No parent-study exposure — H2 is
  a new family; the parent never traded earnings reactions.
- Price basis: split/dividend-adjusted total return throughout.

## Event definition — named before looking at results

(`sts.study.h2_events`, `_PARAM_DEFAULTS`; all constants locked here)

1. **Reaction session R:** for each past report date d, candidates are S0 = first symbol
   session ≥ d and S1 = the next session, in that order; the first whose volume ≥ 1.5× the
   median of the 20 sessions strictly before it is R. Requires ≥ 20 prior sessions; if
   neither candidate qualifies, the report is dropped (never emitted). R must land on a
   master (SPY) session.
2. **Surprise score:** close(R)/close(R−1) − 1 (total reaction, gap + intraday).
3. **Causal decile:** an event's comparison set is every event (any symbol) in the 252
   sessions strictly before its own signal session; requires ≥ 100 comparison events, else
   the event is unflagged (no lookahead, no within-quarter peeking). Flag = "top" at/above
   the 90th percentile of comparison scores, "bottom" at/below the 10th.

## Cells (grid locked; `scripts/run_h2_study.py` CELLS)

1. **top_decile_day2_open** — long top-decile events, enter at the open of R+1.
2. **top_decile_pullback** — descriptive only: resting limit at close(R) scanned 3
   sessions, abort on a close below low(R); known intrabar-ambiguity simplification,
   never used for the verdict.
3. **bottom_decile_day2_open** — descriptive only, the other tail, never traded
   (long-only charter).

**Primary cell (named before any run): `top_decile_day2_open`.** Rationale, from mechanism
and literature priors only: the drift accrues from day 2 onward in the canonical
price-reaction PEAD sorts, so the day-2 open entry is the direct expression of the
documented effect; the pullback entry is a fill-improvement heuristic with a known gap-risk
failure mode (strong drifters never pull back — adverse selection toward the weaker
events), so it earns descriptive status only. No prior run has touched any H2 cell; no
known-prior bias. Secondary cells require a fresh dated prereg to ever become primary.

## Risk & exit parameters

- Stop: `risk.atr_stop(entry, atr14, multiple=2.0)`, ATR taken at the signal bar R
  (shared harness via `h3_events._simulate_event`, config "h2_pead").
- Target: `risk.atr_target(entry, atr14, multiple=2.0)`. Expected R:R shape ~1:1, modest
  win rate above 50% if the drift is real — expectancy, not win rate, carries the read.
- Time stop: 15 sessions, hard (charter constant) — matches the documented drift horizon.
- Position sizing: charter constants not applied — event-level study only, no portfolio
  expression in this prereg.
- Catalyst rule: standard 2-session pre-earnings entry embargo (`block_entry` only). Never
  in tension with H2 by construction: entries occur after the report.

## Two-layer read

- **Layer (a):** exit-free forward returns close(R+h)/close(R) − 1 at h = 5/10/15 on the
  primary cell's OOS events. Must be positive at h=15.
- **Layer (b):** exit-simmed event expectancy on the primary cell, net of 2× friction.

## Bars (locked — judged against the primary cell only)

- [ ] Layer (a): positive raw forward return at h=15.
- [ ] Layer (b): OOS event-level expectancy > 0, net of 2× friction, n ≥ 100 OOS events.
      Below-n is PARK-on-adequacy, never STOP.
- [ ] Year-by-year stability: ≥ 60% of judgeable OOS years positive, ±0.05R neutral zone
      (window holds full 2024 and 2025 plus partial 2026).
- [ ] No single year > ~40% of total edge (over the two full years + partial 2026).
- [ ] Regime slice (SPY above/below 200d) reported; bull-only concentration flagged
      explicitly.
- [ ] Cost sensitivity: verdict recomputed at 2× costs (10bps/side + $2/order); survives =
      robust, dies = fragile — stated explicitly in the verdict.

## Adequacy floors

Minimum n = 30 OOS events per slice cell to be judged; below that, the cell is reported
not-run. Below the study-level n = 100 floor the whole study is PARK-on-adequacy.

## Cost arms

- Base: 5 bps/side + $1/order.
- 2× sensitivity (mandatory): 10 bps/side + $2/order.

## Known caveats

- **Earnings-date quality:** yfinance-sourced dates; the parent found them workable but
  imperfect. The volume-confirmation rule and the S0/S1 candidate window are the built-in
  defenses against off-by-one dates; the event funnel quantifies the drop. A large
  dropped-pre-decile count is a data-quality flag, not a tunable.
- **Price-proxy, not SUE:** the score conflates surprise with reaction; this is the
  standard price-reaction variant, robust in the literature, but not fundamentals-grade.
- Survivorship: feed reflects today's constituents/survivors.
- Regime flattery: bull-skewed window; regime bar is locked.
- Evidence-contract gap (codex_review.md #2) still deferred; layer-b numbers inherit that
  limitation, stated in the verdict if unresolved at run time.

## Verdict rubric

- **PROCEED** → Phase 4 portfolio expression, independent review required first. Maps to:
  all locked bars pass on the primary cell, both cost arms.
- **PARK** → Maps to: layer (a) positive but n < 100 (PARK-on-adequacy); or
  stability/concentration bars inconclusive on the short OOS window; or base-cost arm
  passes but 2× arm fails (fragile, not refuted).
- **STOP** → mechanism refuted: layer (a) h=15 non-positive, or layer (b) OOS expectancy
  negative with n ≥ 100.
